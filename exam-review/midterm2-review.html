Review material for Midterm 2

Math 51 Spring 2022

exam date 2022-04-11

1 Problems

  1. Solve the initial value problem \((D^2 - 6D + 10)x = 0\), \(x(0) = x'(0) = 1\).

  2. Consider the linear ODE \((D^2 - 4)x = e^{2t} + e^{3t}.\)

    1. Find a simplified guess for a particular solution \(p(t)\) for the ODE.

    2. Use the method of undetermined coefficients to find \(p(t)\).

  3. Use the method of variation of parameters to find the general solution to the linear ODE \[(D^2 + 4)x = \dfrac{1}{\sin(2t)}.\]

  4. Consider the linear ODE \((\diamondsuit) \qquad (D^3 + D)x = e^t.\)

    1. Find a matrix \(A\) for which \[(\heartsuit) \qquad D\vec x = A\vec x + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ e^t \end{bmatrix}\] is an equivalent linear system to \((\diamondsuit)\).

    2. If \(u(t)\) is a solution to \((\diamondsuit)\), explain why \(\vec v(t) = \begin{bmatrix} u(t) \\ u'(t) \\ u''(t) \end{bmatrix}\) is a solution to \((\heartsuit)\).

  5. Find the real and imaginary parts of the vector \((\cos(t) + i\sin(t)) \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 2+i\\ 1 \\ i \end{bmatrix}\) for any value of \(t\).

  6. Let \(A= \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}.\)

    1. Find the characteristic polynomial and show that eigenvalues of \(A\) are \(\lambda = 2,-1\).

    2. Find two linearly independent generalized eigenvectors for \(\lambda = 2\). Note that there are not 2 linearly independent eigenvectors for \(\lambda = 2\).

    3. Find the general solution to the linear system \(D\vec x = A\vec x\).

  7. Let \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\)

    1. Find all solutions to the matrix equation \(B \vec v = \vec 0\).

    2. Let \(\vec b_1,\dots,\vec b_5\) denote the columns of the matrix \(B\). Thus the \(\vec b_i\) are vectors in \(\mathbf{R}^4\). Are these vectors linearly independent? Why or why not?

  8. Find the solution \(\vec h(t)\) to the homogeneous system \[D \vec x = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \\ \end{bmatrix} \vec x\] which satisfies \(\vec h(0) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}.\) What is \(\vec h(1)\)?

  9. Suppose that the \(3 \times 3\) matrix \(A\) has eigenvalues \(2\) and \(1 \pm 3i\), that \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2\), and that \(\mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} u_1 + w_1 i \\ u_2 + w_2 i \\ u_3 + w_3 i \end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 1+3i\).

    Describe three real solutions to the homogeneous system of linear ODES \(D \mathbf{x} = A \mathbf{x}\) with linearly independent initial vectors.
